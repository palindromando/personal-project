Title: Ilha das Galinhas Prison Camp Administrative Building

Subject: Galinhas Prison Camp

Date: 12 December 2018

Location: Ilha das Galinhas, Guinea-Bissau

Page(s): 1

Source: Personal Archive Anna Mester

Type: Photo

Description:The Penal Colony of Galinhas Island (Colónia Penal e Agrícola da Ilha das Galinhas) (1936-1974)—While the site of the prison camp on the small island of Galinhas in the Bijagós Archipelago is in ruins and abandoned; the camp lives on in the imaginary of Guineans. The stories of the prison camp on the island of Galinhas persists in Bissau-Guinean popular memory through songs and oral history, but very little is actually known or has been documented about its creation and existence. The exact inauguration is unknown; legislation shows that the first calls for the creation of the agricultural penal camp, officially known as “Colónia Penal Agrícola da Guiné” date back to 1925 in Diploma nº 282 of July 1, 1925. It was not until 1934 when Diploma Legislativo nº 856 of May 7, 1934 stipulated the creation of “four edifices imprisoning locals, underage (under 15) delinquents, inmates from other colonies, and european and assimilated inmates sentenced by the Colonial Tribunals.” In 2018, the ruins of the administrative building still remain. Beneath the main building, there are subterranean prison cells about 3ft (1m) tall, lacking sunlight and infested with bats and snakes. These cells were used for deprivation, torture, and solitary confinement. On the premises, there are remnants of a kitchen, cemetery, and school building. A local resident warned that the cemetery is overgrown with vegetation and infested with snakes. Additionally, he explained that those who were imprisoned (among them poet and musician José Carlos Schwarz) taught local children at the school house. We also spoke with an elderly woman who had traveled to Galinhas Island from Southern Guinea-Bissau with her husband who had been imprisoned at the camp. Her husband had passed and she did not have means to return home, and remained on the island living off the vegetable garden she had cultivated.
